Austin Richard Post (born July 4, 1995),[1] known professionally as Post Malone, is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and record producer.[2] Known for his variegated vocals, Malone has gained acclaim for blending genres and subgenres of hip hop, pop, R&B, and trap. His stage name was derived from inputting his birth name into a rap name generator.

description

Malone began his music career in 2011 and gained recognition with his 2015 debut single "White Iverson",[4] which peaked at number 14 on the US Billboard Hot 100. Malone signed a recording contract with Republic Records[5] and released his debut album Stoney (2016); it contained the diamond single "Congratulations" (featuring Quavo) and set the record for most weeks (77) on the US Billboard Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart.[6] His second album, Beerbongs & Bentleys (2018), debuted at number one on the US Billboard 200 and set several streaming records.[6] It also contained the US number-one singles "Rockstar" (featuring 21 Savage) and "Psycho" (featuring Ty Dolla Sign) and was nominated for Album of the Year at the 61st Grammy Awards.

career

According to Malone, he chose "Post Malone" as his stage name when he was 14 or 15.[22] The name was rumored to be a reference to the professional basketball player Karl Malone,[23] but Malone later explained that "Post" is his last name, and he used a "rap name generator" to get "Malone".[3] At 16, using audio editor Audacity, Malone created his first mixtape, Young and After Them Riches.[24] He showed it to some of his classmates at Grapevine High School.[18][25] He was voted "Most Likely to Become Famous" by his classmates as a senior in high school. He worked at a Chicken Express as a teenager.[18]
